# Models for tracing consequences (ladder rung 3: forward)

Original one-line glosses. Pull a model only when it changes the decision.

- **Feedback loops.** An output that feeds back as input. Reinforcing loops compound; balancing loops pull toward a setpoint. Ask which loop you are standing in.
- **Compounding.** Small advantages, repeated, dominate over time. The levers are the rate and the number of periods.
- **The leak.** The steady outflow from a system. Growth that ignores the leak is a bucket with a hole.
- **Critical mass.** The threshold past which a process sustains itself. Below it, effort dissipates; above it, momentum carries.
- **Emergence.** The whole shows behavior its parts do not. You cannot always read the system off its components.
- **Diminishing returns.** Past a point, more input yields less output, then negative. Find that point before you pour more in.
- **Reciprocity.** Action invites like reaction. Cooperation and hostility both tend to be returned in kind.
- **The adaptation race.** You must keep improving just to hold position, because rivals adapt too. Standing still is falling behind.
- **Selection over time.** Variation plus selection plus time. What persists is what fit the conditions, not what was best in the abstract.
- **Entropy.** Order is not free; left alone, systems drift toward disorder. Maintaining structure takes continuous energy.
- **Inertia.** People and organizations resist changes in motion. Expect resistance in proportion to mass and momentum.
- **Spillovers.** Costs and benefits that land on third parties. The decision looks different once the spillovers are counted.
