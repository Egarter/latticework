# Models for tracing consequences (ladder rung 3: forward)

Original one-line glosses, each with a trigger for when to reach for it. Pull a
model only when it changes the decision.

- **Feedback loops.** An output that feeds back as input. Reinforcing loops compound; balancing loops pull toward a setpoint. Ask which loop you are standing in.
- **Compounding.** Small advantages, repeated, dominate over time. The levers are the rate and the number of periods — ask which one you can actually move.
- **The leak (churn / outflow).** The steady outflow from a system. Before celebrating inflow, measure the outflow: growth that ignores the leak is a bucket with a hole.
- **Critical mass.** The threshold past which a process sustains itself. Ask whether you are above or below it — below, effort dissipates; above, momentum carries.
- **Emergence.** The whole shows behavior its parts do not. When a plan reasons from the parts up, ask what the assembled system will do that no component predicts.
- **Diminishing returns.** Past a point, more input yields less output, then negative. Find that point before you pour more in.
- **Reciprocity.** Action invites like reaction. Expect your move — cooperative or hostile — to be returned in kind, and price that response in now.
- **The adaptation race (Red Queen).** You must keep improving just to hold position, because rivals adapt too. Ask whether standing still here is quietly falling behind.
- **Selection over time.** Variation plus selection plus time. Ask what the conditions are actually selecting for — what persists is what fit them, not what was best in the abstract.
- **Entropy.** Order is not free; left alone, systems drift toward disorder. Budget the continuous energy it takes to hold structure, or expect the drift.
- **Inertia.** People and organizations resist changes in motion. Expect resistance in proportion to mass and momentum, and plan for it.
- **Spillovers.** Costs and benefits that land on third parties. Count the spillovers before deciding — the choice often looks different once they are in the ledger.
