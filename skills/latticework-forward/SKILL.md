# Latticework: Forward

One-shot. Take the move in question and follow it past the first effect.

1. **First order.** What happens immediately and directly? (Usually the obvious,
   intended effect.)
2. **Second order.** And then what? How do the people, incentives, and feedback
   loops touched by the first effect respond to it?
3. **Third order.** And then what? How does the system respond to *those*
   responses? Look for the equilibrium after everyone has adjusted.
4. **Watch for.** Incentive flips, feedback loops that amplify or dampen, and
   the gap between the intended effect and the settled one.
5. **So what.** State how the downstream picture changes the decision, if at all.

Most bad calls are first-order-good and second-order-bad. Find that gap.
